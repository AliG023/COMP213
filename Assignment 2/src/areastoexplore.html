Petticoat Creek Conservation Area, Lake Ontario, waterfront bluffs & trail, swimming pool & splash pad

Black Creek Pioneer Village is a living history museum, the Village at Black Creek offers one of the richest heritage experiences in the Toronto area. You will find historical interpreters and tradespeople in authentic period dress, who will demonstrate and explain for you how people lived, worked and played in mid 19th century rural Ontario.

Lynde Shores Conservation Area Extremely popular for wildlife viewing

Heart Lake Conservation Area offers all the best in outdoor entertainment, offering fishing in the lake or frolicking at our new pool and splash pad, to speeding above the water on one of our twin zip lines.

Heber Down Conservation Area offers a variety of activities including Nature-walks, wetland exploration, and more importantly preserves a portion of the significant Iroquois Beach physiographic unit and is home to a variety of wildlife and plants not common in Durham Region.

Silver Creek Conservation Areahas riches of the land as you wander past babbling brooks, lush mature forests and escarpment rocks. Hike amongst the sights and sounds of nature along forest trails.
